# Psychographic Drift™ — Signal Resolution

## Definition
The gradual shift in audience composition — in urgency or intent — that causes CAC to rise and conversion to fall without any visible explanation.

The profile stays the same.
The state changes.
Performance decays.

Nobody knows why.

---

## Why This Concept Exists

Most SaaS companies use static ICP definitions.

They define the ICP once. They target based on that definition. They do not re-examine it unless results collapse dramatically.

But markets are not static. Buyer states shift over time.

When they do, the same targeting that worked in Q1 no longer works in Q3. The same messaging that converted early adopters no longer converts the next cohort.

From the outside, it looks like:
- A marketing problem
- A messaging problem
- A sales execution problem

It is none of these.

It is Psychographic Drift™.

---

## How Drift Happens

### Scenario 1: Audience Composition Shifts
In the early days, a SaaS company attracts high-urgency buyers — founders actively searching for a solution to an acute problem.

As the company grows and invests in broader acquisition:
- They reach buyers who are less urgent
- More "exploring" buyers enter the pipeline
- The average urgency of the audience drops

CAC rises. Close rate falls. The product hasn't changed. The targeting hasn't changed. But the average buyer state in the pipeline has shifted.

### Scenario 2: Market Conditions Change
External events change the urgency landscape.

Examples:
- A market contraction reduces spending urgency
- A competitor enters with a simpler solution
- An AI tool partially solves the surface problem, reducing urgency for the full solution

The ICP profile looks the same. But the psychographic state across that profile has shifted.

### Scenario 3: Messaging Attracts the Wrong State
Content and messaging that converts early-stage, high-urgency buyers may attract a different state as it scales.

If top-of-funnel content generates curiosity from low-urgency buyers, the pipeline fills with the wrong state.

Volume increases. Conversion rate drops. The team thinks more volume is needed.
More volume makes it worse.

---

## Symptoms of Psychographic Drift™

If you are experiencing more than two of these, drift is likely:

- CAC is rising without a clear cause
- Close rate is declining without change in product or pricing
- Sales cycles are lengthening
- More leads are coming in but fewer are converting
- The "good deals" are becoming harder to find
- The product hasn't changed but results have

---

## How to Detect Drift

### Step 1: Audit Recent Wins vs. Early Wins
Pull the last 10 closed-won deals.
Pull the first 10 closed-won deals.

Compare: are the buyer states the same? Were recent wins driven by the same urgency signals as early wins?

### Step 2: Review Pipeline Language
Look at the language coming from current pipeline.
Is it complaint language (high signal, high urgency)?
Or is it curiosity language (low urgency, early-stage exploring)?

### Step 3: Map to Market State Signals™
Run your current pipeline through the Market State Signals™ classification.
If the urgency distribution has shifted toward Medium/Low — you have drift.

---

## How to Correct Drift

Drift is corrected at the targeting layer, not the messaging layer.

Changing the message to convert lower-urgency buyers is the wrong fix.
It produces longer sales cycles and lower margins.

The correct fix is to re-tighten the Firmographic Gate™ and re-run Psychographic Resolution™ to identify which segments still contain high-urgency buyers.

Then redirect acquisition toward those segments.
