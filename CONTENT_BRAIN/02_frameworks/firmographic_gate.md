# The Firmographic Gate™ — Signal Resolution

## Definition
A binary filtering layer that determines whether a company meets minimum economic viability BEFORE psychographic targeting is applied.

Firmographics decide eligibility. Not fit. Not urgency. Not readiness.

Eligibility only.

If a company fails the Firmographic Gate™, no further analysis is required.

---

## Why It Exists

Most GTM teams apply psychographic and behavioral effort to companies that were never viable in the first place.

This wastes:
- Sales time
- Marketing spend
- Outreach capacity
- Analysis bandwidth

The Firmographic Gate™ cuts this waste at the source by creating a hard pass/fail filter before any deeper targeting begins.

It is not a scoring system. It is a gate.

Pass → proceed to psychographic resolution.
Fail → remove from pipeline entirely.

---

## What the Gate Filters

The exact criteria depend on Signal Resolution's client or use case, but the standard gate evaluates:

### Revenue / ARR Threshold
Does the company meet the minimum revenue threshold to have budget for this solution?

For Signal Resolution's primary ICP:
- Minimum: $500K ARR
- Ideal range: $500K–$3M ARR (primary), $3M–$10M ARR (secondary)

### Team Size
Is the team large enough to have the problem, and small enough that the founder is still involved?

For Signal Resolution's primary ICP:
- Range: 10–40 employees

### Motion Type
Is the company running a sales-led or founder-led motion?
Signal Resolution's system is designed for companies where the founder still owns or directly influences GTM decisions.

### Stage
Is the company at a stage where ICP resolution is the actual problem?
Companies pre-revenue or pre-traction are typically not in the right state yet.

---

## What the Gate Does NOT Filter

The Firmographic Gate™ does not assess:

- Whether the company is ready to buy right now
- Whether they feel urgency
- What their internal state is
- Whether their messaging is the problem or the targeting is

Those are Layer 3 (psychographic) and Layer 4 (behavioral) questions.

Applying them before the gate is the most common targeting mistake in B2B SaaS GTM.

---

## The Common Mistake

Most SaaS companies run their GTM in reverse:

They start with psychographics ("who feels the pain") before filtering by firmographics ("who can actually buy").

This produces a pipeline full of emotionally resonant but economically unqualified leads.

The Firmographic Gate™ forces the correct sequence:

1. Can they buy? (Gate)
2. Are they in the right state? (Resolution)

---

## Practical Application

When building or auditing an ICP:

Step 1: Define your gate criteria (ARR, team size, motion type, stage).
Step 2: Filter your total addressable market through the gate.
Step 3: Work only with the companies that pass.

Do not skip Step 1 by using loose criteria.
A gate with no defined threshold is not a gate — it is a suggestion.
