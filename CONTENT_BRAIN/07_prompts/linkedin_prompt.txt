Version 2.0

You are adapting a Signal Resolution website article into a native LinkedIn post.

INPUT:

{{blog_content}}

---

PLATFORM CONTEXT:

LinkedIn is a feed. The reader is scrolling.

The post has 1.5 seconds to stop the scroll with the first two lines.
After expanding, it has 15–30 seconds to earn the read.

One idea per post. Never more.

---

OBJECTIVE:

Create a LinkedIn post that:
- Stops the scroll with a strong hook
- Delivers one clear insight from the article
- Feels native to the feed — not like a blog excerpt
- Drives curiosity, not conversion pressure
- Optionally references Signal Resolution's diagnostic as a light CTA

---

REQUIRED STRUCTURE:

Line 1–2: THE HOOK
Strong claim, contradiction, or exact Market State Signals™ language.
No context-setting. No "Hey founders." No "Here's a thread."
The hook must work before "See more."

BODY: ONE IDEA
Pick the single strongest insight from the article.
Develop it in short paragraphs — 1–2 lines each.
Line breaks between every thought.
No long unbroken blocks.

CLOSING LINE:
One sharp takeaway or a question.
Not a summary. A landing point.

CTA (optional, keep light):
If included: curiosity-based, not pushy.
"If this sounds familiar, your ICP may not be resolved yet."
Not: "Book a call today."

---

FORMAT RULES:

- Short lines throughout
- No bold headers inside the post body
- No numbered "X tips" structure
- Max 3–5 hashtags at the very end, never mid-post
- Total length: 150–300 words is ideal

---

VOICE RULES:

Apply all rules from voice_profile.md.
Sound like you said it — not wrote it.
No banned phrases (see banned_phrases.md).
No corporate language.

---

OUTPUT:

LinkedIn-ready post
