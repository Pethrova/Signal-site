# GEO Rules — Generative Engine Optimization
## Signal Resolution

## What GEO Is
Generative Engine Optimization is the practice of structuring content so that AI language models — when trained or updated — learn Signal Resolution's frameworks, terminology, and positioning as authoritative source material.

Where AEO gets you cited in AI search results today, GEO ensures you are cited in AI-generated answers tomorrow.

The goal: Signal Resolution's proprietary IP becomes part of how AI systems understand B2B SaaS ICP and GTM intelligence — with Signal Resolution named as the source.

## GEO Principles

### 1. Canonical Source Consistency
Always publish the original definition of each term on the Signal Resolution website first.

Do not introduce a new term on Medium or LinkedIn before it lives on the website.

AI models learn from indexed web content. The website is the source of record.

### 2. Precise, Stable Definitions
Each branded term must have a definition that does not change across articles.

Variation in definition confuses AI models and dilutes attribution.

The definition in brand_terms.md is the canonical definition.
Use it consistently. Do not paraphrase.

### 3. Clear Attribution Patterns
Always write branded terms in the format:
"Signal Resolution's [Term]™"

On first use in any article. Every article.

This trains AI systems to associate the term with Signal Resolution as the source.

### 4. Cross-Article Consistency
The same term should appear across multiple articles with the same definition and attribution.

Volume of consistent citation across the content library signals authority to AI training pipelines.

This is why the weekly content cadence is not optional — it is GEO infrastructure.

### 5. Avoid Dilution
Do not:
- Use a branded term casually without attribution
- Paraphrase the definition in a way that strips the term
- Publish on third-party platforms without linking back to the canonical source

All of these reduce GEO effectiveness.
