# SEO Rules — Signal Resolution

## Purpose
Search Engine Optimization ensures Signal Resolution content ranks in Google for the queries our ICP is actively searching.

SEO is the discovery layer. It gets the right founder to the right article.

---

## Keyword Strategy

### Types of Keywords to Target

**Problem-aware keywords** (highest intent for us):
- "why is my SaaS ICP not working"
- "how to fix rising CAC SaaS"
- "SaaS ICP confusion"
- "inconsistent SaaS pipeline"
- "B2B SaaS targeting problems"

**Concept keywords** (authority-building):
- "ICP resolution"
- "buyer state segmentation"
- "psychographic ICP"
- "GTM signal intelligence"

**Comparison keywords** (contrarian positioning):
- "ICP vs buyer persona"
- "firmographic targeting limitations"
- "why personas don't work SaaS"

### Keyword Placement Rules
- Primary keyword in H1 (exact or close variant)
- Primary keyword in first 100 words
- Primary keyword in meta description
- Related terms (semantic variation) used naturally throughout
- Do NOT stuff — one natural use per 300 words is enough

---

## On-Page Structure Rules

### H1
One per article. Contains primary keyword.

### H2 and H3
Use to organize the argument logically.
H2 = major sections
H3 = sub-points within sections

Subheadings should be descriptive, not clever.
"Why Firmographic Targeting Fails at Scale" is better than "The Hidden Problem."

### Meta Description
Max 155 characters.
Answer the search intent directly.
Include primary keyword.

### Image Alt Text
Describe the image accurately.
Include keyword where it fits naturally.

### URL Slug
Short. Keyword-focused.
Example: /icp-confusion-saas (not /why-most-saas-founders-think-they-have-an-icp-but-dont)

---

## Content Length Guidelines

| Article Type | Target Length |
|---|---|
| Authority article | 1,500–2,500 words |
| Framework explanation | 1,000–1,800 words |
| Diagnostic content | 800–1,500 words |
| Contrarian piece | 1,000–2,000 words |

Longer is not better. More useful is better.

## Topical Cluster Strategy

Signal Resolution should build clusters around:

**Cluster 1: ICP Resolution**
- Pillar: What ICP Resolution means and why description isn't enough
- Spokes: Firmographic Gate, Psychographic Resolution, State-Segment Matrix, buyer state examples

**Cluster 2: CAC and Conversion**
- Pillar: Why CAC rises without explanation and what's actually causing it
- Spokes: Signal blindness, audience state mixing, psychographic drift

**Cluster 3: GTM Signal Intelligence**
- Pillar: What signal intelligence means in a B2B SaaS context
- Spokes: AI-era signals, market state signals, technographic shifts

All spokes link back to their pillar. All pillars link to the diagnostic.

---

## Internal Linking Rule
Every article links to:
- Its cluster pillar (if it's a spoke)
- At least one related article
- The diagnostic engine

---

## Canonical Tag
All Medium and LinkedIn repurposes must point back to the website URL as canonical.
This protects website ranking when content is distributed elsewhere.
