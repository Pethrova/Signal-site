# Audience Stages — Signal Resolution

## Why This Matters
The same founder at Seed and Series A is a different buyer in a different state.

Same profile. Different urgency. Different vocabulary. Different content that converts.

---

## Stage 1 — Seed / Pre-PMF ($0–$500K ARR)

### What They Believe
- The product needs more features
- If the product were better, growth would come
- ICP can wait until they get more data
- They are still figuring out who this is for

### What They Fear
- Running out of runway before finding traction
- Building for the wrong person
- Losing early customers they can't explain

### What They Blame
- The market ("too early")
- The product ("not ready")
- The sales process ("we need someone better")

### What They Are Trying to Fix
- Getting first 10 paying customers
- Understanding who actually buys
- Making the pitch land consistently

### Content That Resonates
- "How to find your real ICP before you run out of runway"
- Diagnostic framing — "do you have this problem?"
- Simple, direct frameworks — not complex systems

### Language They Use
- "We're still figuring out the market"
- "We have a few customers but they're all different"
- "We need to get more data"

---

## Stage 2 — Early Traction ($500K–$3M ARR) ← PRIMARY TARGET

### What They Believe
- They have an ICP (but it was never truly resolved)
- The problem is messaging or traffic
- A better hire will fix it
- Personas are a valid strategy

### What They Fear
- Inconsistent pipeline
- CAC creeping up without explanation
- Not being able to explain why some deals close and others don't

### What They Blame
- Marketing ("we need better leads")
- Sales ("they're not closing")
- Messaging ("it should be working")

### What They Are Trying to Fix
- Making conversion consistent
- Understanding what actually drives a close
- Reducing CAC
- Building a repeatable GTM motion

### Content That Resonates
- Contrarian reframes ("your ICP isn't wrong — it's unresolved")
- Diagnostic content that reveals the hidden layer
- System-level thinking ("here's why this keeps happening")
- Authority content with frameworks they can apply immediately

### Language They Use
- "Nothing is converting"
- "We're getting traffic but no pipeline"
- "We don't know who to target"
- "CAC is rising and we don't know why"
- "Our messaging isn't landing"

---

## Stage 3 — Scaling / Series A ($3M–$10M ARR) ← SECONDARY TARGET

### What They Believe
- Growth exists but is not fully systematized
- They need to scale what's working
- GTM needs to be built into a repeatable process

### What They Fear
- Losing efficiency as they scale
- New GTM hires going in the wrong direction
- ICP drift as they expand segments

### What They Blame
- Lack of process
- Weak handoff between marketing and sales
- Unclear ICP across a growing team

### What They Are Trying to Fix
- Building a GTM intelligence layer
- Giving the GTM team a shared targeting framework
- Moving from founder intuition to signal-driven decisions

### Content That Resonates
- Systems and infrastructure framing
- Intelligence layer positioning ("this is what you build before you scale")
- Data-driven authority content
- Case-level examples of what resolution looks like at scale

### Language They Use
- "We're trying to systematize GTM"
- "We're not sure the team is targeting the right accounts"
- "We need a shared definition of who to go after"
- "We're seeing CAC increase as we scale the team"
