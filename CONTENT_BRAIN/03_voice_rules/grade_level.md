# Grade Level Rules — Signal Resolution

## Target
Grade 4–6 reading level.

This is not about dumbing down. It is about reducing friction between thought and comprehension.

Smart founders don't have time to decode dense prose. Clarity signals confidence.

---

## Rules

### Sentence Length
- Aim for 8–15 words per sentence on average
- Short sentences (under 8 words) are powerful — use them for emphasis
- Long sentences (over 20 words) should be rare
- Never stack three long sentences together

### Word Choice
- Prefer short, common words over long technical ones
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Start" not "initiate"
- "Find" not "identify" (unless being precise)
- "Fix" not "remediate"

### Paragraph Length
- 1–3 sentences per paragraph in most cases
- Web content: lean toward 1–2 sentences
- Never write a 6-sentence paragraph in content meant for the web

### Vocabulary
- Use Signal Resolution's proprietary terms precisely (see brand_terms.md)
- Avoid stacking jargon from multiple fields in the same sentence
- Define any term on first use in long-form content

---

## What Grade 4–6 Is NOT

It is not:
- Childish
- Oversimplified
- Lacking nuance

It is:
- Economical
- Precise
- Fast to read
- Easy to remember

The goal is for a founder to read it at 7am before their first call and absorb it fully.
