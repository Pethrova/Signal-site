# Brand Terms — signal resolution

## Core System Name
ICP Resolution System™

Definition:
A methodology that distinguishes between describing a market and resolving a market into buyable states.

---

## The Resolution Principle™

Definition:
"Description ≠ Resolution. One tells you who they are. The other tells you what state they are in."

Use when:
- Explaining why traditional ICPs fail
- Framing your philosophy
- Introducing your system

---

## The Firmographic Gate™

Definition:
A filtering layer that determines whether a company is economically viable BEFORE psychographic targeting is applied.

Rules:
Firmographics decide eligibility. Not emotion.
Resolution over description. Naming a state is not understanding it.
Signal over noise. Anything not increasing clarity is waste.
Structure over randomness. Systems are the only thing that scales.

---

## Psychographic Resolution™

Definition:
The process of identifying buyer state using real language, not assumptions.

Use when:
- Talking about behavior, urgency, emotion
- Explaining conversion differences

---

## Market State Signals™

Definition:
Real-world language patterns that reveal what a buyer is experiencing internally.

Examples:
- “Nothing is converting”
- “CAC is rising”
- “We don’t know who this is for”

---

## State-Segment Matrix™

Definition:
A model that maps the same ICP across different buyer states.

Core Idea:
Same company ≠ same readiness

---

## Psychographic Drift™

Definition:
When your audience composition shifts in urgency or intent, causing CAC and conversion to change.

Use when:
- Explaining hidden growth problems
- Diagnosing performance issues

---

## Usage Rule

Every piece of content should:
- Use at least 1–2 of these terms naturally
- Connect them to signal resolution
- Reinforce the system as a whole

Avoid:
- Replacing these with generic language
