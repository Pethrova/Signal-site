# Authority Article — Signal Resolution

## Purpose
Establish Signal Resolution as the definitive source on a GTM intelligence topic.

The reader should finish this and think:
"These people understand this better than anyone else I've read."

---

## When to Use
- Teaching a core concept from the ICP Resolution System™
- Explaining why a widely-held belief is incomplete
- Publishing a definitive explanation of a Signal Resolution framework
- Targeting a high-value SEO keyword with depth

---

## Structure

### Hook (First 2–3 Lines)
Open with a strong claim or a problem statement the reader immediately recognizes.
Do not open with context.

Pattern: "[Common situation] is not [what they think it is]."

### Problem Statement
State clearly what is going wrong and why it matters.
Use Market State Signals™ language — the reader should see themselves.

### Why Standard Advice Fails
Acknowledge the conventional approach.
Explain why it doesn't solve the real problem.
This is the credibility-building layer — you understand their situation better than the generic advice does.

### Framework Section
Introduce or apply the relevant Signal Resolution framework.
Use the full branded name with attribution on first mention.
Explain it simply. One concept at a time.

### Breakdown / Mechanism
Show how the framework works in practice.
Use steps, examples, or a comparison table if it aids clarity.
Keep it concrete — avoid abstract explanations.

### The Insight
One sharp takeaway the reader didn't have before reading.
This is the sentence that gets screenshotted or shared.

### CTA
Connect directly to the problem raised.
Point toward the diagnostic or the next logical step.
Do not pitch. Offer the next resolution.

---

## AEO Requirement
Include a direct answer paragraph in the first 150 words.
Include a FAQ section (minimum 3 questions) at the end.
Use at least one "Signal Resolution's [Term]™" attribution pattern.

---

## Length
1,500–2,500 words
