# Educational Piece — Signal Resolution

## Purpose
Teach one concept from the ICP Resolution System™ clearly and completely.

The reader should finish this knowing something they did not know before, and understanding how to apply it.

---

## When to Use
- Introducing a new framework concept to an unfamiliar audience
- Explaining a layer of the ICP Resolution System™ in depth
- Targeting SEO keywords with educational intent ("how to," "what is," "why does")
- Supporting a buyer in State B (aware, moderate urgency) who needs to understand before they act

---

## Structure

### Title
Clear and direct. Should reflect the search intent.
Pattern: "What [Concept] Is and Why It Matters for B2B SaaS GTM"
Or: "How [Process] Works: A Practical Explanation"

### Definition (First 100 Words)
Define the concept simply and directly.
This is the AEO anchor — it must be extractable as a standalone answer.

### Why This Matters
What goes wrong when founders don't understand or apply this concept?
Use concrete consequences, not abstract stakes.

### How It Works
Step-by-step or layer-by-layer explanation.
One idea per paragraph.
Use examples that a seed-to-Series-A founder would immediately recognize.

### Common Mistakes
What do most people get wrong when applying this concept?
This is where the authority layer builds — you understand the edge cases.

### The Better Approach
Show how Signal Resolution's framework handles it correctly.
Use branded terminology with attribution.

### Quick Application
Give the reader one thing they can do today with this knowledge.
Keep it concrete.

### CTA
"If you want to see how this applies to your specific situation, the Signal Resolution diagnostic covers this directly."

### FAQ Section
3–5 questions a founder would actually ask about this concept.
Direct answers. 1–3 sentences each.

---

## AEO Requirement
Strong definition in first 100 words.
FAQ section is mandatory for this content type.
Use structured H3 subheadings throughout.

---

## Length
1,000–1,800 words
