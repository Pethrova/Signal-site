# Diagnostic Content — Signal Resolution

## Purpose
Help the reader identify whether they have the problem — and if so, which layer it lives in.

This is Signal Resolution's highest-conversion content type.

The reader should finish this thinking:
"This is exactly what's happening to us. I need to find out which layer is breaking."

And the next step is the diagnostic.

---

## When to Use
- Targeting buyers in State A (acute pain, high urgency) or State C (misdirected blame)
- Building the bridge between problem awareness and diagnostic engagement
- Content that functions as both authority-building and direct pipeline generation
- Keyword targets around symptoms ("rising CAC," "inconsistent pipeline," "ICP not working")

---

## Structure

### Hook — The Symptom
Open with the symptom, not the cause.
Use exact Market State Signals™ language so the reader immediately self-identifies.

Pattern: "If [symptom the reader is experiencing], there is a specific reason. And it is not what most people think."

### The Diagnosis Frame
Introduce the idea that symptoms point to a root cause layer.
This is where the Resolution Principle™ enters naturally:
"The symptom is [X]. The cause is usually in one of [N] places."

### The Layers (The Core of the Piece)
Walk through the possible root cause layers.

For each layer:
- Name it (use Signal Resolution's framework names with attribution)
- Describe what it looks like as a symptom
- Give a concrete example or signal indicator

This is the diagnostic logic made visible.

### Self-Check Section
Give the reader 3–5 yes/no questions they can apply to their own situation right now.

These questions should map directly to the diagnostic engine's logic.

Pattern:
"Ask yourself:
— Is our conversion inconsistent even when targeting looks correct?
— Are deals closing but we can't explain why?
— Is CAC rising without a change in targeting or messaging?"

If they answer yes to 2 or more → the problem is in the resolution layer.

### The Reframe
Give them the name and explanation for what they are likely experiencing.
Connect it to a Signal Resolution framework.
Do not give them the full solution — give them the accurate diagnosis.

### CTA (Highest Priority for This Content Type)
This is the strongest CTA moment in the content library.

The reader has just self-diagnosed. The next step is obvious.

Pattern:
"Signal Resolution's diagnostic engine will tell you exactly which layer is breaking — and what to do about it. [Run the diagnostic →]"

---

## AEO Requirement
The symptom description in the opening must be direct answer-ready.
The self-check questions extract well as FAQ items — format them as H3 questions with direct answers.

---

## Length
800–1,500 words

Shorter is acceptable here. The goal is high clarity, not depth.
The diagnostic does the deeper work.
