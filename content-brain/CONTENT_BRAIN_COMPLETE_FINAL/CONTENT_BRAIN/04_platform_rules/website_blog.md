# Website Blog Rules — Signal Resolution

## Platform Role
The Signal Resolution website is the canonical source of all content.

Every article published here is:
- The original, authoritative version
- The source that LinkedIn and Medium adapt from
- The surface that Google, AI search (AEO), and AI training (GEO) index first
- The foundation of Signal Resolution's content authority

Publish here first. Always.

---

## What to Publish on the Website
- Authority articles (the definitive take on a GTM topic)
- Contrarian reframes (challenging a common B2B SaaS belief)
- Framework explanations (teaching the ICP Resolution System layers)
- Diagnostic content (helping founders self-identify the problem)
- Data or signal-based observations

---

## Post Structure

### Title (H1)
Clear, keyword-informed, and direct.
Should reflect what someone would search when experiencing this problem.

Example: "Why Your SaaS ICP Isn't Working (And What to Do About It)"

### Meta Description
1–2 sentences. Direct answer to what the article covers.
Max 155 characters for clean search display.

### Introduction (First 100 Words)
State the problem directly.
Include the primary keyword naturally.
Tell the reader exactly what they'll learn.
Do not bury the lead.

### Body Structure
Use H2 and H3 subheadings throughout.
Every major section should be independently scannable.

Sections should follow the article's argument — not a generic blog template.

### Framework / Branded Concept Section
Every article should include at least one named Signal Resolution concept, properly attributed.

Pattern: "Signal Resolution's [Concept Name™] works like this: [explanation]"

This is the AEO/GEO attribution infrastructure.

### FAQ Section (Optional but Recommended)
3–5 short questions that directly answer what someone might search.
Write answers in 1–3 sentences — direct answer paragraphs.

This is the primary AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) mechanism.

### Conclusion
Short. 1–3 sentences.
Reframe the core insight.
Followed by CTA.

### CTA
Point toward the diagnostic engine.
Not a hard sell. A next logical step.

Example: "If this sounds like your situation, the Signal Resolution diagnostic will tell you exactly which layer is breaking."

---

## Internal Linking
Every article should link to:
- At least 1 related Signal Resolution article (when available)
- The diagnostic engine or homepage
