# LinkedIn Rules — Signal Resolution

## Platform Behavior
LinkedIn is a feed. Readers are scrolling.

Your content has 1.5 seconds to stop the scroll.
After that, readers decide in 3 seconds whether to expand.

Do not write for the full post. Write for the first two lines.

---

## Content Purpose on LinkedIn
- Build authority and recognition among seed-stage SaaS founders
- Introduce or reinforce Signal Resolution's frameworks
- Drive curiosity toward the diagnostic, not toward a hard sell

---

## Post Structure

### Line 1–2: The Hook
Strong claim, contradiction, or real problem statement.
No context. No "Hey SaaS founders." No "Here's a thread on..."

Bad: "Understanding your ICP is crucial for growth. Here's what I've learned."
Good: "Most SaaS founders think they have an ICP. They don't."

### Body: One Idea Only
Develop one insight per post.
Do not try to teach everything.
Do not include multiple frameworks.

Use:
- Short paragraphs (1–2 lines each)
- Line breaks between every thought
- No long unbroken blocks of text

### Closing Line
One sharp takeaway or question.
Make them think. Do not summarize.

### CTA
Optional. Keep it soft.

Good: "If this sounds familiar, your ICP may not be resolved yet."
Bad: "Book a call with us today."

---

## Format Rules
- No numbered lists that look like "10 tips" posts
- No bold headers inside the post body
- Max 3–5 hashtags — at the end, not mid-post
- One idea. One direction. One landing point.

---

## Recommended Post Types
1. Contrarian insight — challenge a common belief
2. Framework micro-teach — one concept, simplified
3. Real problem → reframe — start with a founder complaint, reveal the hidden cause
4. Diagnostic observation — "If you're seeing X, the cause is usually Y"

---

## Tone on LinkedIn
More casual than a blog. Less academic.
Sound like you said it, not wrote it.
