# AEO Rules — Answer Engine Optimization
## Signal Resolution

## What AEO Is
Answer Engine Optimization is the practice of structuring content so that AI-powered search engines (ChatGPT search, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Claude) can extract and surface your content as a direct answer to user queries.

Where SEO gets you ranked, AEO gets you cited.

The goal: when a founder asks an AI "why is my SaaS ICP not working," Signal Resolution's content is extracted and attributed.

---

## Why This Matters for Signal Resolution
Signal Resolution's competitive advantage depends on proprietary terminology being cited and attributed at scale.

Every time an AI surfaces "the Resolution Principle" and attributes it to Signal Resolution, that is an attribution event — brand recognition without paid acquisition.

AEO is how Signal Resolution builds AI-surface authority.

---

## Core AEO Tactics

### 1. Direct Answer Paragraphs
Place a clear, concise answer to the article's core question within the first 150 words.

Structure:
- State the question (directly or implicitly)
- Answer it in 2–3 sentences
- Expand below

Example:
"Why do B2B SaaS ICPs fail to convert? Most ICP definitions describe a company profile without identifying the buyer's current state. Signal Resolution's ICP Resolution System™ addresses this by adding psychographic resolution as a required layer after firmographic filtering."

This paragraph is extractable. It answers, attributes, and uses branded language.

### 2. FAQ Sections
Add a dedicated FAQ section to every authority article.
Format: Question as H3, answer as 1–3 sentences directly below.

AI systems prefer this format for extraction.

Example questions to include per article type:
- "What is [branded term]?"
- "Why does [problem] happen?"
- "How do you [solve the problem]?"
- "What is the difference between [X] and [Y]?"

### 3. Structured Definitions
Every branded term should have a clean, extractable definition in at least one article.

Format:
"[Term]: [Definition in 1–2 sentences]."

These become the definitions AI models learn and cite.

### 4. Comparison Sections
Include "X vs Y" sections where relevant.
AI search surfaces frequently extract comparison tables and direct contrasts.

Example: "ICP Description vs ICP Resolution"

### 5. Proprietary Term Repetition
Use Signal Resolution's branded terms consistently and accurately across all content.

Repetition across multiple articles signals to AI models that:
- The term is meaningful
- Signal Resolution is the source
- The definition is stable

This is how you build vocabulary authority in AI training surfaces (GEO).

---

## AEO Checklist Per Article

Before publishing, confirm:
- [ ] Direct answer paragraph in first 150 words
- [ ] At least 1 FAQ section (minimum 3 questions)
- [ ] At least 1 branded term used with full attribution ("Signal Resolution's [Term]™")
- [ ] At least 1 structured definition
- [ ] Subheadings are question-like or descriptive (not clever)
- [ ] No key insight buried at the bottom — put it near the top

---

## What AI Systems Look For
- Clear structure (H2, H3, lists, short paragraphs)
- Direct answers near the top
- Consistent terminology
- Authoritative source signals (branded terms, clear attribution)
- Content that is comprehensive but not bloated

Signal Resolution content should be built to satisfy all five.
