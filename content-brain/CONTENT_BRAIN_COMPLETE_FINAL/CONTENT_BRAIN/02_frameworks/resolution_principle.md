# The Resolution Principle™ — Signal Resolution

## Definition
Description ≠ Resolution.

Description tells you who a company is.
Resolution tells you what state they are in.

Only state drives conversion.

This is Signal Resolution's foundational principle. Every other framework layer builds from it.

---

## The Problem It Solves

Most B2B SaaS companies build their ICP by describing their best customers:

- Industry: SaaS
- Size: 10–50 employees
- Role: Founder or VP Sales

This feels like clarity. It is not.

Two companies can match that description perfectly and behave completely differently.

One converts immediately.
One disappears after the demo.

The profile did not predict this.
The state would have.

---

## Why Description Fails

Description is static. It captures what a company looks like at a moment in time.

State is dynamic. It captures what a company is experiencing right now.

Buying decisions are not made from profiles.
They are made from states — urgency, pressure, clarity, fear of staying stuck.

A description-first ICP targets the right profile in the wrong state.
That is why conversion feels inconsistent even when targeting "looks right."

---

## What Resolution Adds

Resolution requires identifying:

1. What problem are they actively experiencing?
2. How urgently do they need it solved?
3. Are they blaming the right layer?
4. What language reveals their internal state?

These questions cannot be answered by firmographic data alone.
They require Market State Signals™ — real language, real behavior, real urgency indicators.

---

## The Practical Difference

| Description-First ICP | Resolution-First ICP |
|---|---|
| "B2B SaaS, 10–50 employees, founder-led" | "Founder-led SaaS with rising CAC and no clear conversion explanation" |
| Static. Describes who they are. | Dynamic. Describes what state they're in. |
| Targets everyone who could buy | Targets those who are ready to buy now |
| High volume, inconsistent conversion | Lower volume, higher close rate |

---

## When to Apply This Principle

Use the Resolution Principle™ as the reframe whenever:

- Conversion is inconsistent despite correct targeting
- The ICP "feels right" but performance is weak
- A founder claims to know their ICP but cannot explain why their best deals close
- Messaging is being blamed for a targeting problem
