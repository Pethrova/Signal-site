## Strong Hooks

### Contrarian Opening
"Most SaaS founders think they have an ICP. They don't. They have a description."

### Problem Recognition Opening
"If your conversion is inconsistent and you can't explain why, the problem is not the message. The problem is the layer beneath the message."

### State-Based Opening
"Two founders. Same industry. Same team size. Same product. One closes 40% of pipeline. The other closes 12%. The difference is not the pitch. It is the buyer state they are targeting."

### Blunt Reframe
"CAC does not rise because leads are bad. CAC rises because you are targeting the right profile in the wrong state."

### Diagnostic Hook
"There is a specific reason your pipeline is inconsistent. It is almost never the one your team is blaming."

## Strong Framework Explanations

### Resolution Principle (Simple Version)
"Description tells you who they are. Resolution tells you what state they are in. Only state drives conversion. This is the Resolution Principle™ — the foundation of everything Signal Resolution builds."

### Firmographic Gate (Practical Version)
"The Firmographic Gate™ is not a scoring model. It is a binary filter. You either pass or you don't. If a company can't buy, no amount of psychographic targeting matters. The gate stops wasted effort before it begins."

### Psychographic Drift (The Explanation That Lands)
"Your ICP didn't change. Your results did. This is Psychographic Drift™ — the gradual shift in buyer state across your audience that raises CAC without any visible cause. The profile is still right. The urgency composition has shifted."

### Market State Signals (Why They Matter)
"The most valuable data you have is not in your CRM. It is in the language your market is using publicly right now. 'Nothing is converting.' 'We don't know who this is for.' These are not complaints — they are state indicators. Signal Resolution calls them Market State Signals™."

## Rhythm Examples (Sentence Variation)

### Short-Medium-Short Pattern
"Most SaaS founders think they have an ICP.
They can describe it in detail — industry, company size, job title.
They don't have it.
They have a description."

### The Landing Paragraph
"Signal Resolution's Resolution Principle™ makes the distinction clear: description tells you who they are, but resolution tells you what state they are in. Same profile. Different state. Different outcome. This is why conversion is inconsistent even when targeting looks correct."

### The Mechanism Explained
"The Firmographic Gate™ runs first. Not because firmographics predict intent — they don't. Because they predict eligibility. If a company cannot buy, psychographics are irrelevant. The gate separates the analysis that matters from the analysis that wastes time."
