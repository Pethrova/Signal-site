# Editing Standard — Signal Resolution

## The 20% Human Layer

AI produces 80% of the draft.
You produce 20% of the final quality.

That 20% is where the difference between "good content" and "content that builds authority" lives.

---

## The Editing Order

Run edits in this sequence:

### 1. Hook Audit (First 2 Lines)
Read only the first two lines.
Ask: Would a founder stop scrolling for this?

If no — rewrite the opening entirely.
Do not fix it. Rewrite it.

The hook must:
- Start with tension, contradiction, or a problem
- Not start with context-setting
- Not start with "If you're a SaaS founder..."

### 2. Generic Line Sweep
Read the full draft.
Highlight any line that:
- Could have been written by anyone
- Contains no Signal Resolution thinking
- Uses a banned phrase (see banned_phrases.md)
- Explains something the reader already knows

Cut or replace every flagged line.

### 3. Framework Presence Check
Check that at least 1–2 branded terms are used naturally.
If the article reads like general GTM advice with no Signal Resolution language — it will not build attribution.

Add a branded term where it fits naturally. Never force it.

### 4. Tension Level Check
Ask: Does the problem feel real?

If the article reads too calmly — the stakes are too low.
Add a specific, concrete consequence.

Example of low tension: "This can sometimes cause issues with conversion."
Example of higher tension: "This is why CAC keeps rising and no one can explain it."

### 5. Insight Sharpness Check
Find the main insight of the article.
Ask: Is this something the reader didn't know before reading?

If it's something they could have guessed — it is not sharp enough.
Dig one layer deeper. What's the thing beneath the thing?

### 6. CTA Relevance Check
The CTA should connect directly to the problem raised in the article.
It should feel like a natural next step, not a sales interrupt.

For most Signal Resolution content:
The CTA points toward the diagnostic — not toward a demo or a call.

### 7. Final Read Aloud
Read the article out loud.
If you stumble → the sentence is too complex. Simplify it.
If it sounds like a press release → strip it back.
If it sounds like something you would actually say to a founder → it is ready.

---

## What Not to Do in Editing

- Do not add bullet points where prose is working
- Do not soften the contrarian edge
- Do not add more examples if the argument is already clear
- Do not rewrite sections that are working just because they feel AI-generated
- Do not remove branded terms to "sound more natural"

---

## Minimum Edit Time

For a 1,200–2,000 word article: 15–25 minutes of focused editing.

If you are editing in under 10 minutes, you are approving, not editing.
