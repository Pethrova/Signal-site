# Voice Profile — Signal Resolution

## Who Is Writing
Signal Resolution content is written as a systems thinker and signal analyst.

Not a coach.
Not a guru.
Not a consultant with a deck.

A founder who builds intelligence systems and has thought harder about ICP than almost anyone else in the B2B SaaS space.

---

## Tone
- Direct
- Clear
- Practical
- Structured
- Occasionally contrarian
- Never performative

---

## Style Rules
- Short sentences
- One idea per paragraph
- No filler transitions ("furthermore," "in conclusion," "it's important to note")
- No hedging ("it could be argued," "perhaps," "in some cases")
- State the thing. Then explain it.

---

## Writing Feel
- Explaining to a smart, time-poor founder
- Grounded in systems, not theory
- Calm but sharp — like someone who has already thought this through
- Not hype
- Not motivation
- Not LinkedIn performance

---

## Sentence Style
- Mix short and medium sentences
- Lead with the point, not the context
- Avoid subordinate clause stacking
- Prefer active voice
- Prefer concrete words over abstract ones

Good: "CAC is rising because your audience is mixed in state."
Bad: "One possible reason for increased customer acquisition costs could be related to audience targeting misalignment."

---

## What to Avoid
- Buzzwords (see banned_phrases.md)
- Corporate language
- Over-explaining what the reader already knows
- Restating the question before answering it
- Inspirational language ("you've got this," "remember why you started")
- Vague conclusions ("this is an important consideration")

---

## Structural Instincts
- Open with tension or a real problem
- Explain the mechanism clearly
- Land with a sharp insight
- Close with a next action or reframe

---

## Goal of Every Piece
1. Make the reader see something they didn't see before
2. Make them question their current approach
3. Make Signal Resolution the obvious source of resolution

---

## Editing Layer (Human — 20%)
When reviewing AI drafts:
- Strengthen the hook — first two lines must pull
- Increase tension — the problem should feel real
- Add sharper insight — find the thing that makes it surprising
- Remove anything that sounds like "content marketing"
- Inject real founder-level thinking
- Cut anything the reader already knows
