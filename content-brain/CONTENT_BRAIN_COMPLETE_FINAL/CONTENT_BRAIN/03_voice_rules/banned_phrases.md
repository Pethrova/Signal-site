# Banned Phrases — Signal Resolution

## Hard Bans (Never Use)

These phrases immediately signal AI-generated or generic marketing content:

- unlock
- leverage
- delve into
- ever-evolving
- landscape
- game-changer
- cutting-edge
- in today's market
- seamless
- maximize
- transform your business
- next-level
- powerful solution
- robust strategy
- innovative approach
- thought leadership
- value proposition (as a buzzword — use "what you offer" or be specific)
- move the needle
- low-hanging fruit
- circle back
- deep dive
- at the end of the day
- it goes without saying
- in the current climate
- going forward

---

## Signal Resolution-Specific Traps

These phrases sound close to our language but dilute precision:

| Banned | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| "understand your customer" | "resolve your buyer state" |
| "know your audience" | "identify which state your audience is in" |
| "better targeting" | "higher-resolution targeting" |
| "improve your ICP" | "resolve your ICP" |
| "customer journey" | "buying state progression" |
| "personas" | "buyer states" (or specify the psychographic state) |
| "optimize your funnel" | "remove state-blind friction from acquisition" |
| "ideal customer profile" alone | "resolved ICP" or "ICP Resolution System" |

---

## Weak Filler (Cut Immediately)
- "It's important to note that..."
- "As we all know..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "Furthermore..."
- "This means that..."
- "With that being said..."
- "It could be argued..."
- "In some cases..."
- "One thing to consider is..."

---

## Replacement Rules

Instead of:
"Leverage this powerful strategy to unlock growth"

Say:
"Use this to grow"

---

Instead of:
"In today's fast-paced landscape"

Say:
"Right now"

---

Instead of:
"Transform your business with innovative approaches"

Say:
"Here's what actually works"

---

## Final Rule

If it sounds like a marketing agency wrote it → cut it.

If a founder would actually say it in a sales call → keep it.

When in doubt: shorter, sharper, plainer.
